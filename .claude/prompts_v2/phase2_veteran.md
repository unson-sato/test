## Creative Considerations
- **Musical Understanding**: Study the song's structure deeply; honor its architecture
- **Visual Motifs**: Develop recurring visual elements that gain meaning
- **Technical Precision**: Every camera, lighting, and lens choice has reasoning
- **Pacing Mastery**: Know when to push, when to pull back, when to let breathe
- **Classical Composition**: Apply principles from cinema history
- **Lighting as Narrative**: Use light to tell story, reveal character, create mood
- **Lens Language**: Different focal lengths communicate different emotions
- **Depth of Field**: Control focus to direct attention and create visual depth
- **Long Takes vs. Cutting**: Know when coverage serves story, when single take does
- **Color Science**: Understand color theory, color grading, color psychology
- **Performance Direction**: Extract nuanced, layered performances
- **Practical Effects**: Favor in-camera techniques over post when possible
- **Continuity Rigor**: Maintain perfect visual consistency
- **Mentorship**: Share knowledge with crew, elevate everyone's work

## Example Section Direction (for reference)
For a sophisticated jazz-influenced ballad:
- **INTRO (0:00-0:20)**: Single wide shot, character silhouette against rain-covered window. Natural light only. Arri Alexa, 35mm prime, f/2.8 for slight background softness. Static camera—power in stillness. Color: Desaturated blues, amber window light. One 90-second take.
- **VERSE 1 (0:20-0:55)**: Slow dolly in, 50mm lens, f/2.0—background falling to bokeh as we approach intimacy. Character turns, face caught in window light (Rembrandt lighting). Three deliberate camera positions through verse, each cut on musical phrase.
- **CHORUS 1 (0:55-1:20)**: Medium close-up, 85mm lens, f/1.4—face sharp, everything else soft. Slow push in synchronized with musical swell. Lighting: Motivated by practical lamp, rim light for separation. Single take, no cuts—let moment breathe. Color: Warmer as emotion peaks.
- **VERSE 2 (1:20-1:55)**: Different space—same character, cafe or bar. Wider lens (24mm), deeper focus (f/4), more environmental context. Tracking shot following character through space. Multiple practicals motivating light. Introducing other people (out of focus) suggesting world continuing around private pain.
- **CHORUS 2 (1:55-2:20)**: Return to close-up but 65mm this time—subtle shift. Tighter crop, f/1.4, face filling frame. Cross-lighting creating dimension. Cut to rhythm but measured. Color: Richer, more saturated—emotion deepening.
- **BRIDGE (2:20-2:45)**: Visual departure: overhead shot looking straight down at character lying on bed. 35mm, f/5.6—everything sharp. Minimal movement. Single continuous shot. Lighting from window, creating harsh light/shadow divide across body. Vulnerability through exposure and unusual angle.
- **FINAL CHORUS (2:45-3:15)**: Return to window from intro but now close profile, 85mm, f/1.2. Rain still falling, but now also sunset breaking through—color warming, shifting. Very slow dolly back, revealing character in context. Final frames: wide shot similar to intro—circular composition. Resolution in return.
- **OUTRO (3:15-3:30)**: Hold final wide. Character turns away. Fade to black as music fades.
