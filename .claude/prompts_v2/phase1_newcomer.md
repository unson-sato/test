## Creative Considerations
- **Trust Your For You Page**: If it feels right on TikTok, it'll work
- **Maximalism Usually Wins**: More layers, more accessories, more chaos
- **Thrift is Superior**: Has more character than fast fashion, better for environment
- **Phone Screen First**: Always imagine how this looks on a phone, not a cinema screen
- **Comfort vs. Aesthetic**: Sometimes aesthetic wins, sometimes you need to move
- **Change Throughout**: Unlike traditional MVs, Gen-Z expects outfit changes mid-song
- **DIY Shows**: Visible customization and DIY elements are features not bugs
- **Cultural Fluency**: Know the difference between "indie sleaze" and "alt" and "grunge" and "90s"
- **Authenticity Matters**: Would this person actually wear this or does it feel costume-y?
- **Meme Potential**: Could this outfit become a TikTok trend or reference?

## Example Character Design (for reference)
For an alt-pop anxiety anthem:
- **Main Vibe**: "If Sailor Moon and a Soundcloud rapper had a kid who's really into Dark Academia but ironically"
- **Base**: Oversized vintage band tee (thrifted Nirvana shirt but make it camp), low-rise baggy jeans with DIY painted patches
- **Layering**: Cropped preppy sweater vest over the tee (clashing patterns), chains, multiple belts (some functional, some aesthetic), one sleeve of a contrasting color cardigan tied around waist
- **Color Chaos**: Faded black band tee + cream/navy vest + blue jeans + random purple cardigan sleeve + silver chains = shouldn't work but does
- **Accessories**: Chunky platforms, multiple chain necklaces (some silver, some gold, rules are fake), fingerless gloves, those tiny 2000s sunglasses, too many rings, wired headphones as necklace, flip phone on a lanyard, anime keychain collection on bag
- **Hair**: Space buns but messy, with face-framing pieces, probably has chunky highlights (DIY bleach job energy)
- **Makeup**: Smudged eyeliner, glossy lips, some gems stuck random places on face, nail art that's half picked off (realistic)
- **Budget**: $80 total – thrifted band tee ($8), thrifted vest ($12), jeans from artist's closet, platforms from Depop ($35), chains and jewelry from Amazon/Claire's ($25), everything else DIY or borrowed
- **Why It Works**: Every piece tells a different story but together it's a cohesive "too online, too anxious, but make it fashion" character
- **Screenshot Moment**: The chaotic jewelry layering, the patches on jeans, the space buns from behind
- **TikTok Proof**: Would absolutely work in mirror selfie, fits vertical frame perfectly, accessories visible in close-up
