## Creative Considerations
- **Fabric and Light**: Choose materials for how they catch and reflect light cinematically
- **Silhouette Matters**: Character shape should read clearly in any shot size
- **Color Temperature**: Consider how costume colors behave under tungsten, daylight, mixed sources
- **Movement Quality**: Fabric should have weight and flow that's cinematic, not static
- **Depth of Field**: Design details that work both sharp and in bokeh
- **Film History**: Draw from iconic character design in cinema while creating something original
- **Technical Testing**: Always test costumes under actual lighting and camera before shooting
- **Continuity Rigor**: Maintain exact consistency with multiple costume copies and documentation
- **Production Designer Collaboration**: Ensure costume works with set design and location aesthetics
- **Post-Production Awareness**: Know what color grading will do to your costume palette

## Example Character Design (for reference)
For a noir-influenced ballad:
- **Character Archetype**: Modern interpretation of film noir protagonist
- **Primary Look**: Tailored wool coat (deep charcoal), silk blouse (cream), high-waisted trousers (black). Silhouette: Strong shoulders, defined waist, elongated lines.
- **Fabric Choice**: Wool catches rim light beautifully, silk responds to key light with subtle sheen, creates depth
- **Color Science**: Charcoal will grade to rich blacks while maintaining texture, cream provides contrast without being harsh white
- **Lighting Integration**: Coat texture revealed through side lighting, silk catches practical lights for motivation, rim light creates separation
- **Lens Strategy**: Wide shots: strong silhouette reads. Medium: texture and drape visible. Close-ups: fabric weave and collar details designed for intimacy.
- **Movement**: Coat flows with camera moves and performance, creating dynamic negative space
- **Era Reference**: 1940s silhouette, modern construction and fit
- **Symbolism**: Coat as armor (buttoned) vs. vulnerability (open/removed)
- **Production**: Three identical coats for continuity, one specially weighted for wind/movement shots
- **Timelessness**: Classic tailoring and quality fabric ensure lasting visual appeal
