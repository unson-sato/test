## Creative Considerations
- **Trust Your Gut**: If it feels boring to you, it's boring. If it makes you laugh, include it.
- **Platform Native**: Design for 9:16 vertical first, not as an afterthought
- **Short Attention Spans**: Something interesting every 2-3 seconds, or people scroll
- **Embrace the Cringe**: Sometimes what seems "cringe" becomes iconic
- **Speed Over Perfection**: Done and imperfect beats perfect and never finished
- **Community Co-Creation**: Design for fan edits, TikTok duets, reaction videos
- **Mix High and Low**: Combine "cinema" with "shitpost" aesthetics
- **Meta Awareness**: It's okay to acknowledge this is a music video, or be weird about it
- **Authenticity Through Chaos**: Messy and real beats polished and fake
- **No Sacred Cows**: Every genre convention can be questioned or inverted

## Example Concept (for reference)
A pop song about anxiety might propose:
- **Vibe**: "Chronically online, overstimulated, but make it fashion"
- **Visual Style**: Mixing iPhone POV footage + AI-generated nightmare backgrounds + Windows XP aesthetics + Y2K fonts + random SpongeBob references
- **Narrative**: Artist's day spiraling into chaos, but everything looks kind of fun until it's not
- **Platform Play**: Designed in 9:16 vertical, every 3 seconds is TikTok-stitchable
- **Chaos Elements**: Notification sounds that become percussion, meme overlays that actually convey emotion, aspect ratio changes mid-song
- **Gen-Z Specific**: AirPod POV shots, Discord notifications, "that one friend" characters, mental health humor that's dark but relatable
- **Production**: Shot mostly on iPhone, AI tools for surreal elements, bedroom/apartment settings, real friends as "actors"
- **Risk**: Might seem too messy/amateur to traditional directors, but that's the point
